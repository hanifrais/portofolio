Data Description

The dataset consists of 13,318 rows and multiple columns spread across four tables, offering a comprehensive view of sales performance and customer insights:

  • Products Table: Includes details about Product ID, Category, Sub-Category, and Product Name to analyze product sales and profitability.
  • Order Table: Tracks transactional data such as Order ID, Order Date, Ship Mode, Customer Segment, Sales, Quantity, Discount, and Profit for sales analysis.
  • Location Table: Provides geographical details like Postal Code, City, State, Region, and Country/Region for regional performance analysis.
  • Customer Table: Contains Customer ID and Customer Name for customer segmentation and behavior analysis.

Initial Questions

  • a. What are the year-over-year trends for sales, profit, and quantity metrics?
  • b. Which product subcategories drive the most and least sales and profits?
  • c. What are the weekly trends in sales and profit performance, and how can outliers (high and low-performing weeks) be explained?

Objectives

  • a. Understand Year-over-Year Performance: Identify growth patterns in sales, profit, and quantity compared to the previous year.
  • b. Analyze Subcategory Contributions: Evaluate sales and profit performance by product subcategories to identify top and bottom performers.
  • c. Examine Weekly Trends: Analyze weekly sales and profit trends to identify high and low-performing weeks and their contributing factors.

Insights

a. Year-over-Year Insights

  • a.1: Sales increased by 20.59%, with a significant 43.72% profit growth, indicating better cost management or higher margins.
  • a.2: Quantity growth (+26.83%) is consistent with sales growth, suggesting stable demand for products.

b. Subcategory Insights

  • b.1: High performers like Phones, Binders, and Storage likely contribute disproportionately to revenue and profit.
  • b.2: Low performers (Fasteners, Labels, Supplies) might have low demand or offer minimal profit margins, requiring reevaluation of their portfolio or pricing.

c. Weekly Trends Insights

  • c.1: Early weeks and Weeks 21 and 30 show below-average sales, likely due to low seasonal demand or operational inefficiencies.
  • c.2: Late-year weeks, especially Week 49, outperform average sales, likely driven by holiday season demand or targeted campaigns.

Recommendations

  • From Insight a: Focus on sustaining profit margins while driving sales volume further through targeted promotions and bundling strategies in growing segments.
  • From Insight b:
    • Increase marketing and promotion for top-performing subcategories like Phones, Binders, and Storage to maintain momentum.
    • Assess the viability of low-performing subcategories (Fasteners, Labels, Supplies) and consider repositioning, discontinuing, or redesigning these products.
  • From Insight c:
    • Implement demand-forecasting models to anticipate low-performing weeks and introduce targeted discounts or campaigns during these periods.
    • Capitalize on high-demand periods, such as late-year weeks, by ensuring inventory readiness, staffing optimization, and promotional offers tailored for the holiday season.
