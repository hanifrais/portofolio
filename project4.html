4. Insights

a. Attrition Insights

The overall attrition rate stands at 16.12%, with 237 employees leaving the organization. This rate is relatively high and could indicate potential issues within the employee retention strategy.

b. Gender, Department, and Age Insights

Gender: Male employees exhibit higher attrition (150) compared to females (87).

Department: Research & Development (R&D) has the highest attrition rate at 56.12%, indicating that employees in this department are more likely to leave. On the other hand, HR has the lowest attrition rate, suggesting stronger retention strategies or a more satisfied workforce in that department.

Age group: The highest attrition rate occurs in the 18.14% age group, comprising 112 employees. This age group may face different retention challenges, such as career development opportunities or work-life balance issues.

c. Education and Job Satisfaction Insights

Education Field: Employees from the Life Sciences field exhibit the highest attrition rate (89 employees). This suggests that employees in this area may be facing unique challenges or dissatisfaction with their roles.

Job Satisfaction: Employees in Research (80) and Sales Representative (83) roles report the lowest job satisfaction scores. This correlates with higher attrition rates in these roles, indicating that dissatisfaction is a significant driver of employee turnover in these departments.

5. Recommendations

  • Develop and implement a company-wide retention strategy to reduce the attrition rate below 12% over the next 12 months. Focus on improving work-life balance, career growth, and employee engagement.
  • Implement mentorship and career development programs for male employees and staff in the R&D department to reduce their attrition rate by at least 20% within six months.
  • Create tailored career paths and offer developmental opportunities to employees in the 18.14% age group to improve engagement and reduce attrition by 15% within the next year.
  • Conduct in-depth focus groups with employees from the Life Sciences field to identify specific pain points contributing to their high attrition. Aim for a 15% reduction in attrition within this segment over the next year.
  • Launch job satisfaction improvement initiatives in the Research and Sales Representative roles, including training programs, performance incentives, and regular surveys, to reduce attrition by at least 10% within the next six months.
