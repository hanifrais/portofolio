1. Executive Summary

The report analyzes the attrition rate, employee satisfaction, and demographic patterns within a healthcare organization. The analysis reveals that the overall attrition rate is 16.12%, with key factors including gender, department, and education field contributing to higher turnover rates. The insights are crucial for the organization to address retention challenges and improve employee satisfaction.

2. Data Description

2.1 Dataset Overview

The dataset consists of key HR metrics, including attrition, employee demographics, job satisfaction, department, education, and other performance indicators. The dataset comprises:

  • Employee Metrics: Attrition, gender, job role, department, and education field.
  • Job Satisfaction Metrics: Satisfaction rates for different job roles and departments.
  • Demographic Data: Age, education level, and overtime hours.

2.2 Key Metrics Used

  • Attrition Rate: Total attrition and active employee count.
  • Job Satisfaction: Satisfaction scores by department and job role.
  • Department-wise Attrition: Attrition rates by department.
  • Age Group Analysis: Attrition by age group and education field.

3. Objective

The analysis aimed to understand the employee attrition rate, identify key drivers for employee turnover, and provide actionable insights to improve retention and satisfaction across different employee segments.

4. Key Results

Overall Attrition and Demographic Insights

The overall attrition rate is 16.12%, with 237 employees leaving the organization. The highest attrition occurs in the R&D department (56.12%), and the lowest attrition is observed in HR. Male employees exhibit higher attrition (150) compared to females (87).

Attrition by Age and Education Field

The highest attrition rate of 18.14% is found in a specific age group, with 112 employees affected. Employees from the Life Sciences field also show higher attrition (89 employees), suggesting a possible misalignment with job satisfaction or career progression.

Job Satisfaction Insights

Job satisfaction scores are lowest in Research (80) and Sales Representative (83) roles. These roles have a higher likelihood of attrition, indicating dissatisfaction as a key driver of turnover.

5. Recommendations

  • Enhance Retention Strategies: Focus on improving retention strategies for male employees and those in the R&D department, aiming for a 20% reduction in attrition within the next 12 months.
  • Career Development Programs: Introduce mentorship and career development opportunities for employees in the 18.14% age group and those from the Life Sciences field, aiming to reduce attrition in these segments by 15% within the next year.
  • Improve Job Satisfaction: Implement satisfaction improvement initiatives in Research and Sales Representative roles, including better support, training, and recognition programs, with the goal of increasing satisfaction scores by 10% within the next six months.
