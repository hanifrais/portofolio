1. Executive Summary

This analysis highlights the key factors driving employee attrition and provides actionable insights to improve retention. Attrition rates, satisfaction metrics, and demographic trends are evaluated to identify at-risk groups and implement targeted strategies for improvement.

2. Data Description

The dataset includes 20 columns, providing comprehensive insights into demographics, work attributes, and satisfaction metrics:

  • Employee Data: Age, Gender, Education Field, Job Role.
  • Job Attributes: Department, Over Time, Job Level, Training Times Last Year.
  • Satisfaction Metrics: Job Satisfaction, Environment Satisfaction.
  • Attrition Indicators: CF_attrition label, CF_current Employee.

3. Initial Questions

  • What is the overall attrition rate and how does it impact the organization?
  • How does attrition vary by gender, department, and age group?
  • Which factors, such as education field and job role, are most associated with attrition?

4. Objectives

  • Attrition Overview: Understand the overall attrition rate, number of active employees, and demographic trends.
  • Gender, Department, and Age Analysis: Evaluate attrition patterns across gender, department, and age groups to identify at-risk employee segments.
  • Education and Job Role Impact: Analyze how education fields and job roles influence attrition and satisfaction levels.

5. Insights

  • Attrition Insights: The overall attrition rate stands at 16.12%, with 237 employees leaving the organization.
  • Gender, Department, and Age Insights: Male employees and the R&D department show the highest attrition rates.
  • Education and Job Satisfaction Insights: Employees in Life Sciences fields and roles like Research and Sales Representatives report the lowest satisfaction scores.

6. Recommendations

  • Develop a company-wide retention strategy focusing on work-life balance and career growth to reduce the attrition rate below 12% within 12 months.
  • Implement mentorship and development programs for at-risk groups, targeting male employees and the R&D department.
  • Conduct focus groups and launch job satisfaction initiatives for low-performing roles to reduce attrition by at least 10% in six months.
